## ‚õΩ The Fuel Paradox

### Chemical Rockets: Hopelessly Inadequate

**The Problem:**
- Chemical reactions release limited energy
- **Specific impulse:** ~450 seconds (hydrogen-oxygen)
- **Exhaust velocity:** ~4.4 km/s
- **Maximum achievable speed:** ~0.001% light speed

**Fuel Requirements for Alpha Centauri (10% c):**

To accelerate 1,000 kg to 30,000 km/s with chemical rockets:

```
Mass ratio = e^(Œîv / v_exhaust)
Mass ratio = e^(30,000 / 4.4)
Mass ratio = e^6,818
Mass ratio = 10^2,963
```

This is **not a typo**. You would need:
- **10^2,963 kg of fuel** for every kg of spacecraft
- **More mass than exists in the observable universe**

### Even Nuclear Fusion Fails

**Fusion advantages:**
- 0.7% mass-energy conversion (vs. 0.0000001% for chemical)
- **Specific impulse:** ~100,000 seconds
- **Exhaust velocity:** ~1,000 km/s

**Realistic fusion spacecraft:**
- **Reactor mass:** Tens of tons (magnetic confinement, shielding)
- **Fuel:** Deuterium, tritium, helium-3
- **Mass ratio for 10% c:** Still ~1,000:1

**The rocket equation wins:**
```
Œîv = v_exhaust √ó ln(m_initial / m_final)
```

Every gram of fuel requires more fuel to accelerate it. The math is **unforgiving**.
